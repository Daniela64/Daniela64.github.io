Final Project



For my final project for Art 74, I tapped further into the digital art of 3D printing. Based on the Rushton dolls of the 1950s, I wanted to put my own dynamic twist on both the doll itself and the message behind it. With the body of a sheep and the face of a wolf, you find this new approach to the term “A wolf in sheep's clothing”. With a cute, soft plush exterior on the outside, it seems like a person you can trust and believe in, and wouldn’t try to purposely harm you. But on the inside, represented by the wolf face, you find a hard interior that is deceptive and distrustful, a person who doesn’t have the best intentions for you, and who constantly goes around with a facade. The suit on the plush body is a ploy on our current president, representing how both him and the system can appear as a wolf in sheep's clothing to the people who truly believe in what he says, but don’t understand that they has no good intentions for us.

The Body:

Step 1: I bought a sheep stuffed animal for the base of the whole project


Step 2: I traced the sheep body on some newspaper and placed the template on my fleece fabric


Step 3: I cut out the template and realzed I would have to sew the arms separate so I cut those off too


Step 4: I separated my pieces and aligned them to where I would be sewing them


Step 5: I sewed the body part of the suit and flipped it inside out so the soft side was on the outslide


Step 6: I did the same as before with the first sleeve


Step 7: I sewed both sleeves on the body and I had what originally looked like a blue sweater


Step 8: I put the sweater onto the sheep and cut it down the center turning it into a jacket


Step 9: I glued on a piece of white fabric for the shirt in the center of his stomach


Step 10: I added all the suit details like a collar on the jacket and shirt and a red tie



The Face:


Step 1: I learned blender and sculpted my wolf face with three spheres


Step 2: after trial and error I re-adjusted the face to be a bit wider and flat so the eyes would look even


Step 3: I went through three draft prints before I got my final face printed


Step 4: I then sanded the print down until it felt smooth and there were no lines


Step 5: I covered the whole face in bondo and let it dry within a minute


Step 6: I then sanded the bondo down until smooth


Step 7: Once I was finished with the sanding I began with my first base layer of grey on the face


Step 8: I then began adding all of the details like the yes nose, tongue, tooth and mouth line


Step 9: After I had finished that I realized I needed to add eyebrows because the face was a bit flat without it


Step 10: Once I was finished with the face I cut the original face off of the sheep


Step 11: I finally added the new printed face onto the sheep and I was finished.
